The Complete Jackery Buying Guide

Everything you need to know to pick the right portable solar generator for camping, RV, home backup, or off-grid life.

Step 1: Know Your Power Needs

Before choosing a Jackery, figure out what you need to power and for how long. Add up the wattage of your devices and multiply by the hours you want to run them.

Common Device Wattages

  • Smartphone charge: 5–20W
  • Laptop: 45–100W
  • CPAP machine: 30–60W
  • Mini fridge: 60–100W
  • LED lights: 5–15W each
  • Box fan: 40–100W
  • Coffee maker: 600–1200W
  • Electric blanket: 100–150W
  • Window AC unit: 500–1500W

Step 3: LiFePO4 vs Standard Lithium-Ion

Jackery's newer v2 and Plus models use LiFePO4 (LFP) batteries. Here's why that matters:

LiFePO4 (v2/Plus models) — Recommended

  • 3,000+ charge cycles (10+ year lifespan)
  • Safer — no thermal runaway risk
  • Performs better in cold weather
  • Charges faster with ChargeShield technology

Standard Lithium-Ion (older models)

  • 500–1,000 charge cycles
  • Still reliable and proven
  • Usually lower price point
  • Good option if budget is the priority

Step 4: Solar Generator vs Power Station Only

A Solar Generator bundles a power station + solar panels together — better value if you know you want to solar charge. A Power Station Only lets you add panels later and charges via wall or car charger right now.
